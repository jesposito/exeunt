Cross-Origin Frames

If a course iframe is hosted on a different domain from the LMS (common with CDN-hosted content), Chrome's same-origin policy will block script injection into that frame.

Workaround: Open DevTools, click the frame context selector at the top of the Console tab, switch to the course iframe, and paste the completion script from the repository directly into the console.

SCORM API Shim Detection

Exeunt searches for the API in these locations, in order:

  • window.API
  • window.top.API
  • window.parent.API
  • window.pipwerks.SCORM.API.handle
  • window.API_1484_11 (SCORM 2004)
  • window.top.API_1484_11
  • window.parent.API_1484_11

Why this tool exists

Against Compliance Theatre

Compliance eLearning is one of the most expensive broken promises in the modern workplace. Hundreds of billions spent globally each year on courses that treat adults like suspects, conflate clicking with understanding, and measure seat time instead of capability.

"The map is not the territory. The completion certificate is not the competence."

What bad eLearning looks like

  • 47-slide PowerPoint-to-SCORM conversions locked behind mandatory "Next" buttons
  • Knowledge checks that punish incorrect answers rather than teach through them
  • Mandatory completion of irrelevant modules for role-based curricula
  • Annual re-completions of content that hasn't changed because the LMS calendar said so
  • Completion certificates as the primary measure of organisational capability
  • Courses that exist to satisfy auditors, not develop people
  • Minimum time-on-slide locks that prevent you from advancing even if you already know the answer
  • Unskippable video introductions by an executive who won't remember filming them

What it costs

Time. The one resource that can't be recovered. Every hour a capable professional spends clicking through content they don't need is an hour not spent doing the actual work. Multiply that by your headcount. Multiply that by your annual mandatory curriculum. That's not a learning investment — it's a tax on productivity dressed up in instructional design language.

It also costs trust. When the learning function is experienced as something done to employees rather than for them, you create a culture where compliance checkboxes are gamed, screen-recording tools proliferate, and the entire system of organisational development loses its legitimacy.

Who this is for

  • L&D professionals who need to test courses without sitting through 45 minutes of content for every test run
  • Developers debugging SCORM implementations without navigating an entire course to reach the commit sequence
  • Learners who already demonstrably hold the knowledge being assessed and shouldn't have to prove it by clicking slides
  • Anyone who finds themselves doing mandatory compliance training for the seventh consecutive year on a topic they could teach

The counterargument (and why it's mostly wrong)

"But the completion record exists for a reason — regulatory requirements, liability, audit trails." This is true, for a small subset of training. Genuine safety-critical knowledge checks for high-stakes roles have real justification. A 12-module induction on company values that every employee does regardless of experience or role does not.

The regulatory argument is also routinely used to justify far more than regulations actually require. Most auditors care that a completion record exists. They rarely care about the quality of the learning experience that preceded it. Exeunt makes the record exist. The LMS is satisfied. The auditor is satisfied. The hour you didn't waste belongs to you.

What good looks like

Learning that respects the learner — short, targeted, performance-focused, available on demand, designed to actually change behaviour, and structured around demonstrated outcomes rather than passive consumption. Learning where completion means something because the experience was worth completing.

If you're building that: you probably won't need this tool much. If you're navigating anything else: Exeunt is yours.

Exeunt is not a bypass for safety-critical training where demonstrated competence genuinely matters. Use your judgment. You probably know which is which.
